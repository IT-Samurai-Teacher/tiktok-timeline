Enhanced ByteDance Timeline

2012
ByteDance Founded

ByteDance was founded by Zhang Yiming and Liang Rubo in Beijing.

Toutiao, ByteDance's first app, was launched as a news aggregator powered by AI, quickly gaining popularity in China.

2016
Douyin Launched

ByteDance launched Douyin, a short-video app, exclusively for the Chinese market.

Douyin leveraged advanced AI algorithms to personalize user content, laying the groundwork for TikTok's global success.

2017
TikTok Launched

TikTok was launched globally, revolutionizing short-form video content.

ByteDance acquired Musical.ly in 2017 for $1 billion and merged it with TikTok to expand its presence in the U.S. market.

2020
CapCut Released

ByteDance launched CapCut, a video editing app that quickly gained popularity among TikTok creators.

CapCut's easy-to-use editing tools made it a favorite for TikTok content creators worldwide, contributing to TikTok's ecosystem.

2025
TikTok Faces U.S. Ban

TikTok suspended services in the U.S. following a government mandate to divest ByteDance's ownership.

Although TikTok temporarily restored service after the incoming administration signaled a 90-day extension, its future in the U.S. remains uncertain.
