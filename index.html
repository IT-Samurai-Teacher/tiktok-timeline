The TikTok Revolution

ByteDance's Innovations and Challenges

2012
ByteDance Founded

ByteDance was founded by Zhang Yiming in Beijing. It started as a news aggregator company, showing early signs of powerful AI-driven content recommendation systems.

2016
Apps Launched: Douyin (China) and TikTok (Global)

ByteDance launched Douyin in China, followed by TikTok for international audiences. TikTok became the first app to take short-form video global.

2018
Acquired Musical.ly

ByteDance acquired Musical.ly and merged it with TikTok, enhancing its global appeal.

2019
Jianying and Toutiao Apps

ByteDance introduced Jianying (a video editing app) and Toutiao (a popular news aggregation app in China).

2020
U.S. Ban Concerns

The U.S. government raised national security concerns over TikTok, citing ByteDance's Chinese ownership. Discussions of potential bans began.

January 19, 2025
TikTok Removed from App Stores

TikTok was removed from major U.S. app stores due to national security concerns, disrupting service for millions of U.S. users.

January 2025
Proposed Resolution

The President-elect proposed a plan to restore TikTok operations by mandating 50% U.S. ownership.
