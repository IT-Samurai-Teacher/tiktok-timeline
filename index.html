ByteDance Timeline

Key Innovations and Challenges

2012
ByteDance Founded

ByteDance was founded by Zhang Yiming and Liang Rubo in Beijing.

ByteDance's first app, Toutiao, an AI-powered news aggregator, was launched in August.

2016
Douyin Launched

ByteDance launched Douyin, a short-video sharing app in China.

In December, ByteDance acquired a controlling stake in the Indonesian news app BABE.

2017
TikTok Launched

TikTok was launched globally, followed by the acquisition of Musical.ly in November.

2020
U.S. Ban Concerns

The U.S. government raised national security concerns about TikTok, citing its Chinese ownership.

In September, Oracle and Walmart announced a deal to become TikTok's technology partners in the U.S.

2025
TikTok Suspends Service in the U.S.

On January 18, TikTok suspended services in the U.S. following a failed divestiture deal.

Service was restored on January 19 after the incoming administration signaled a 90-day extension.
